Philippine Art History

The Spoliarium - Juan Luna

Painted in 1884, this masterpiece won the first gold medal at the Exposición Nacional de Bellas Artes in Madrid. It serves as a powerful symbol of Filipino social, moral, and political life during the Spanish colonization.

The Pre-Colonial Era

Before Spanish arrival, Philippine art was seen in ritual pottery (like the Manunggul Jar), intricate weaving patterns, and gold jewelry. Art was deeply tied to community, spirit, and nature.

Fernando Amorsolo

Known as the "Grand Old Man of Philippine Art," Amorsolo was the first National Artist. His work celebrated the Philippine sun and rural life, defining the Filipino aesthetic for generations.
